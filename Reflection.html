Rob

Once again, I personally believe we have designed and created a beautifully presented, informative, clean and very professional website and PDF document. I am extremely proud of the results we have achieved as a group of green IT students. In comparison with our efforts from the previous project, while the groups last project was top class from top to bottom, I believe we have improved on our html programming and our information acquisition skills to produce a visually stunning, clean and easy to navigate website and have also produced some high quality work regarding the description of our project of choice. While our previous project was well planned and presented, there were still aspects of our team work skills that needed to be worked on. These were primarily our time management and group communications skills. After completing the last assignment, we discussed and agreed to begin work as early as possible for this assignment. This time around, we jumped straight into the massive project description section of the assignment choosing our desired roles and workloads almost instantly. This created a great foundation on which we began building our document. While our time management and communications were not perfect and truly never will be, the early start and early progress allowed large amounts of downtime, real world work obligations and personal down time for life outside our studies. While this has had a profound and positive impact on our ability to create quality work within our desired timeframes, our time management can always be better and our group discussions can always be more frequent. In an industry where those two skills are paramount and of the utmost importance, learning that there is no such thing as perfect time management and perfect communications is a vital and important lesson. Even still, I was personally surprised by how much quality work we were able to achieve, while also correctly and wisely managing our time for work on our other projects for our IT course.
