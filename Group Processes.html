Group Processes

While our team was able to construct a PDF document and webpage for assignment 2 with the highest quality possible, there were definitely times when the team could have been working together more efficiently and effectively. The biggest hinderance to our completion of the very large workload set before us was quite simply our enthusiasm in starting the project within a reasonable time. While some members were quick to begin construction of the PDF and the group webpage, others left their work until the very last minutes, causing high stress, anxiety and frustration to the rest of the group. In order to counter these teamwork problems during work on the last assignment, some members took it upon themselves to do more than their fair share of the work just so that the project would be completed on time and with high quality. After writing our team reflection and SparkPLUS critiques, we came together as a group to organise some more effective ways of producing our projects in the future.

After having constructive conversations with the team upon project submission we were all in agreement that there should be some changes for the next few team projects. One of these changes was the continuation of a team leader for the projects, however this time they would be operating as the team leader from the origin of the project. This would prove to greatly increase our team's efficiency and effectiveness across the lifespans of the remaining group projects. Another highly important change we all agreed to make was to choose our workloads and begin working on these workloads at our earliest conveniences. Beginning our development of our PDF and webpage as early as possible would be crucial in the later weeks of the session as multiple projects and assignments were now due across multiple subjects and the only way to properly deal with the large quantities of work required was by starting early on as many necessary projects as possible. Being time efficient from the beginning would ensure that our workloads would not get out of control and push unnecessary stresses and strains onto the other group members.

Lastly, one other extremely important factor in building and maintaining great team work is great team communication. Thankfully our group has been in contact with one another since the group's inception and have continued to be productive in regards to team communications. This ongoing communicative effectiveness will continue to enable our team to resolve almost all potential work-related issues in the future before they become large and stressful problems.
